Issue Breakdowns

Seven Structural Problems
Reducing Lead Generation Performance.

01

Critical Priority · Above the Fold

Weak Above-the-Fold Messaging

The Problem

Generic headline creates zero differentiation

The current headline reads "Quality Roofing Services You Can Trust" — identical to every competitor. Within 3 seconds a visitor cannot identify who this is for, what problem it solves, or why this company is different. The scan fails before the first sentence is read.

Strategic Fix

Rewrite around a specific client problem and outcome

Replace with: "Roof replacements completed on schedule — no delays, no surprises, guaranteed workmanship for 10 years." Specific, differentiated, and addresses the real buyer fear.

02

Critical Priority · Action Architecture

Weak CTA Structure

The Problem

Single vague CTA with no hierarchy

One "Contact Us" button — in the nav and once below the fold. Non-specific, low-intent, and requires commitment before trust has been established anywhere. No secondary lower-friction option exists.

Strategic Fix

Two-tier CTA: primary offer + secondary option

Primary: "Get a Free Roof Assessment" — specific, benefit-oriented. Secondary: "See Our Recent Projects" — lower commitment for undecided visitors. Both placed above the fold.

03

Critical Priority · Trust Infrastructure

Trust Signals Are Buried

The Problem

Reviews and credentials placed after the decision point

Three testimonials appear on scroll 4+. Licensing, insurance, years in operation are absent above the fold. Visitors form their impression — and leave — before encountering a single trust signal.

Strategic Fix

Trust strip below headline, testimonial above CTA

Compact trust strip immediately below headline: star rating, review count, licensing status. Strongest testimonial placed directly above the primary CTA button to eliminate hesitation at the action point.

04

High Impact · Mobile Experience

Mobile Experience Creates Friction

The Problem

Desktop layout forced into mobile without restructuring

Hero image compresses to illegibility. CTA button shrinks below accessible tap target size (44px minimum). Phone number appears in small footer text and is not click-to-call. Navigation requires horizontal scrolling on smaller screens.

Strategic Fix

Mobile-first conversion redesign

Sticky call button at top of mobile viewport. Full-width CTA at minimum 48px. Simplified hero: text-dominant with one clear action. Phone number as a prominent tappable element throughout.

05

High Impact · Attention Architecture

Weak Visual Hierarchy

The Problem

Every element has equal visual weight

Same font sizes, same spacing, same contrast ratios throughout. No hierarchy directing attention toward headline, trust signals, or CTA. The page reads as a flat wall of equal-weight information — the eye has no path to follow.

Strategic Fix

Establish a deliberate 3-tier visual hierarchy

Headline at 2–3× body size. CTAs with significantly higher contrast. Supporting information visually subordinate. The visitor's eye should follow: headline → trust signal → CTA with no ambiguity about sequence.

06

Standard Priority · Lead Infrastructure

No Lead Capture Layer

The Problem

All-or-nothing: full contact form or leave

Visitors who are not ready to request a quote have no intermediate option. No guide, no assessment offer, no lower-commitment path. The funnel is binary — and roughly 85% of visitors exit the wrong side.

Strategic Fix

Add a mid-funnel lead capture offer

"Download our Roofing Replacement Cost Guide" or "Get our 10-point Roof Assessment Checklist" — captures email from not-yet-ready visitors and feeds them into a nurture sequence that converts on a longer timeline.

07

Standard Priority · Conversion Journey

No Structured Conversion Journey

The Problem

Section order follows no buyer decision logic

Services → About → Gallery → Testimonials → Contact. This order is informational, not persuasive. A visitor arriving with a problem is not guided from recognition through trust-building to action. Each section feels static rather than progressive.

Strategic Fix

Restructure to mirror buyer decision sequence

(1) Problem recognition + positioning, (2) Trust evidence, (3) Process clarity — "what happens next", (4) Social proof, (5) Low-friction action. This mirrors how buyers actually decide — reducing resistance at each stage.

Priority Action

The Highest-Leverage Changes
Would Be Structural, Not Cosmetic.

FIX 01 — Critical

Rewrite Above-the-Fold Messaging

Replace generic positioning with a specific, problem-focused headline. Single highest-leverage change — impacts every subsequent decision the visitor makes on the page.

FIX 02 — Critical

Two-tier CTA system — specific primary action, lower-commitment secondary — placed above the fold and repeated at each trust-building milestone down the page.

FIX 03 — Critical

Add Trust Reinforcement Near Action Points

Compact trust strip immediately below headline. Strongest testimonial directly above CTA. Credential indicators adjacent to action buttons to eliminate hesitation.

Strategic Takeaway

Most Service Business Websites
Underperform for Structural Reasons.

Improving lead generation performance usually requires more than redesigning visuals. The roofing website analysed here is not aesthetically broken — it is structurally broken. The visual treatment is adequate. The conversion architecture is not.

The highest-performing websites function as integrated systems: trust systems, conversion systems, visibility systems, and lead qualification systems working in sequence. Without that structure, traffic alone rarely creates reliable growth.
