The Core Problem

Issue Breakdowns

Six Structural Failures
Reducing Visibility and Trust.

01

Critical · Business Description

Weak Business Description Messaging

The Problem

Generic copy names no services, areas, or trust signals

The current description reads like a template: "quality service," "customer satisfaction," and "free quote." It communicates nothing specific about what makes this business the right choice and wastes the 750-character field that Google uses as a relevance signal.

Strategic Fix

Rewrite using service specifics, location, and outcome language

Name the specific services (roof replacement, storm damage repair, flat roofing). Name the service areas. Include a trust signal (years in operation, number of projects completed). Close with a clear next action. This simultaneously improves SEO relevance and buyer confidence.

02

Critical · Trust Infrastructure

Missing Trust Reinforcement

The Problem

4 reviews, zero owner responses, no credentials

Review count of 4 is too low to signal established authority to either Google or buyers. Zero owner responses miss the opportunity to demonstrate responsiveness, add keywords, and build relationship signals. Business attributes and credentials are absent.

Strategic Fix

Build a review acquisition and response system

Implement a systematic post-job review request sequence. Respond to every review within 24 hours using specific, keyword-rich language that reinforces service areas and quality standards. Set all relevant business attributes and credential badges.

03

Critical · Visual Presence

Poor Visual Optimisation

The Problem

2 photos uploaded: No cover image, no team photos, no completed work.

Profiles with fewer than 10 photos receive significantly lower engagement and click-through rates. The two photos uploaded are low resolution and unrelated to completed work. Without a cover photo, Google selects a random image to display, which is often unflattering.

Strategic Fix

Upload 20+ categorised, high-quality images

Minimum portfolio: 5 before/after project photos, 3 team shots, 2 vehicle/equipment photos, 1 business premises, 1 logo, and a cover image showing the best completed project. Images signal active operation and allow buyers to assess quality before calling.

04

High Impact · Social Proof

Weak Review Structure

The Problem

4 reviews with no response strategy or volume system

Google's local algorithm weights review velocity, or the consistent acquisition of new reviews, above total review count. Four reviews with a 4.1-star rating signal a dormant business. Buyers who scan reviews before calling see insufficient social proof to act confidently.

Strategic Fix

Systematic post-job review request with response protocol

Automated SMS and email review requests sent 48 hours after job completion. Use an owner response template for every review, whether positive or negative, with specific service-related language embedded. Target: A minimum of five new reviews per month.

Why It Hurts

Review velocity directly affects local pack ranking

Google treats consistent new review activity as a signal of an active, legitimate business. Businesses with stagnant review profiles, even highly rated ones, tend to drift down in Local Pack rankings over time.

Expected Impact

Improved ranking position + higher click-through

Businesses with 40+ reviews and consistent velocity regularly outrank competitors with higher ratings but fewer total reviews. CTR from local pack improves with both star rating and review count simultaneously.

05

High Impact · Activity Signals

No Consistent Activity Signals

The Problem

Zero posts in 90 days: Profile appears inactive.

Google Posts, Q&A responses, and profile updates are freshness signals. A profile with no activity for more than 90 days suggests the business is either closed or disengaged. This reduces both its authority in local ranking calculations and buyer confidence.

Strategic Fix

Weekly post cadence + populated Q&A section

One GBP post per week minimum: alternate between project showcases, service spotlights, seasonal offers, and educational content. Pre-populate Q&A with the 6 most common buyer questions and detailed, keyword-rich answers. This signals active operation to both Google and buyers.

06

Standard Priority · Intent Alignment

Weak Local Intent Alignment

The Problem

Single broad category: No service-area pages or secondary categories

The profile is listed under a single general category ("Roofing Contractor") with no secondary categories, no service-area specification beyond a broad radius, and no service-specific pages linked. This reduces visibility for high-intent, specific searches like "flat roof repair [city]" or "emergency roof leak [postcode]."

Strategic Fix

Secondary categories, service areas, and linked pages

Add secondary categories (Roof Inspection, Storm Damage Repair, Waterproofing Contractor). Specify service areas by town/postcode. Link Google Posts to specific service landing pages on the website. This builds intent-specific visibility across a wider local search surface area.

Priority Actions

The Biggest Visibility Gains Come From
Trust Reinforcement and Profile Structure.

FIX 01

Stronger Messaging

Rewrite the business description using specific services, named service areas, trust evidence, and credentials. Use the full 750-character field. Include keywords that match buyer search intent.

FIX 02

Visual Trust Signals

Upload 20+ categorised, geo-tagged images. Set a strong cover photo. Before/after project evidence is the single highest-converting visual element on a GBP profile.

FIX 03

Review Optimisation

Build a systematic post-job review request workflow. Respond to every review within 24 hours using keyword-rich, specific language. Target 5+ new reviews per month with consistent velocity.

FIX 04

Consistent Activity Signals

Post weekly GBP updates. Populate Q&A with the 6 most common buyer questions. Add secondary categories and service-area pages to improve intent-specific search visibility.
